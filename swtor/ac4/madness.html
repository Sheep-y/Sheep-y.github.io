Madness Sorcerer: The Classic Sith
Basics
  • Lighted robed, lightsaber wielding, seeks wisdom and help over action.
  • Story highlights: Recover artifacts, form alliances and armies as a Sith Master, join the Sith Council, make a holocron.
  • Spec: Ranged, DoT DPS, Sith (Force-user), off-heal.
  • Pros: mobility, self heal, team support.
  • Cons: few defences, lack of burst.
Notes
  • Hardest Sorcerer discipline, excels at kiting and long fights.
  • Stable rotation, zero proc dependency, very reliable relic proc.
  • Lightning is Sorcerer's burst DPS spec, easier to play and shares same gear.
  • Corruption is Sorcerer's heal spec, very versatile and efficient, but uses different gear.
  • Republic counterpart: Balance Sage.
Rotations
Elle's Very Simple Rotation
Basics
  1. Demolish (Pre-cast/instant)
  2. Creeping Terror
  3. Affliction
  4. Death Field (Spread DoT)
  5. Force Leach
  6. Force Lightning (Full cast)
  7. Lightning Strike
  8. Force Lightning (Full cast)
  9. Force Lightning (Clipped, throw vanquish asap)
Notes
  • When dummy parsing, simply repeat it over and over.
  • You can move around during steps 1 to 4 (or 1 to 5 with utilities); find a safe place to throw lightnings.
  • If rotation can't keep up with Demolish's cooldown, skip the last Force Lightning.
Expanded Rotation
Basics
  • (Open) (If mid-fight & Demolish not primed) Force Lightning
  • (Off) Polarity Shift
  • 0.0s Demolish / Mind Crush (Pre-cast/instant)
  • 1.5s Creeping Terror
  • 3.0s Affliction
  • (Off) Recklessness
  • 4.5s Death Field (Spread DoTs at lv. 28+)
  • (Off) Unnatural Preservation
  • 6.0s Force Leach (Shock before lv. 40)
  • 7.5s Force Lightning (Full cast)
  • 9.6s Lightning Strike (Instant) or Resurgense
  • (Off) Cloud Mind
  • 11.1s Force Lightning (Full cast)
  • 13.2s Force Lightning (Clipped; skip before lv 56.) or bubble
  • 14.7s Next rotation.
Notes
  • Everything after first Force Lightning are fillers, but it's best to do one more Force Lightning to maintain the buffs.
  • Off-GCD ability is best queued after instant ability, before GCD finishes, together with next ability for lowest delay.
  • Polarity Shift must be before Demolish for maximum throughput.
  • Use Unlimited Power after Polarity Shift for boss or burn phase.
AoE Rotation
Basics
  • Creeping Terror or Affliction
  • (Optional) Affliction or Creeping Terror
  • Death Field (DoT spread)
  • Force Storm
  • Force Storm
  • Switch to single target
Notes
  • Use experience to judge whether to put one DoT or two, and which.
  • Death Field is our heaviest AoE, equals a full Force Storm at one DoT, slightly above at two.
  • If the situation allows, use Force Push to push opponents together for Death Field.
Abilities

Main Attacks
Basics
  • Dealing damage is our main job.
  • Basic Attack: Force Lightning (regen Force).
  • Use on cooldown: Demolish, Death Field, Force Leach.
  • Never hardcast Demolish except to start a fight.
Force Lightning
Basics
  • Zero-cooldown basic attack, good damage and give us force, but can't use on move.
  • 4 hits (a full channel) make next Demolish and Lightning Strike instant cast and do higher damage at less force (last 15s).
  • Each hits also buff DoT crit chance by 2% (max 8%, last 10s).
Usages
  1. Whenever we can stay still and big powers are on CD; priming Demolish is very important.
  2. Finish off near death trash mobs and adds.
Notes
  • Ticks immediately and then twice per second, 4 ticks total.
  • Immune to pushback.
  • Hits does not need to be continuous for the buffs; can run, hit twice, run, hit twice.
  • Each tick will consumes a Recklessness, which is very wasteful.
  • At level 40, each tick has 20% chance to trigger Rippling Force which immediately deals around half tick damage and can crit.
Demolish
Basics
  • Our biggest attack, good upfront (burst) damage, heaviest and quickest tick. (tick per 1s)
  • Always use after primed by Throw. 25% damage on our biggest attack.
  • Make target vulnerable 5% to Force damage.
Usages
  1. Hardcast to start a combat. This is an exception to no hardcast rule.
  2. Use on cooldown. Force Lightning often and save the buff to be used on cooldown.
Notes
  • Ticks once per seconds for 9 seconds, 9 ticks total after upfront damage.
  • Upfront damage is not DoT tick and does not enjoy DoT buffs.
  • Balance Sage counterpart: Vanquish.
Creeping Terror
Basics
  • Long lasting DoT, spreadable by Death Field.
  • Pin victim on a hidden cooldown of 9 seconds.
Usages
  1. Whenever the target is not going to fall in a few seconds or so.
  2. DoT spreading by Death Field or manually.
  3. To start a fight on a melee foes when its friends are ranged, so that they stay together for easy DoT spread.
  4. To prevent kiting opponent from closing or jumping on us.
Notes
  • Ticks immediately and once per 3 seconds for 18 seconds, 7 ticks total.
  • Deals internal (yellow) damage, which bypass many defences.
  • Cost only half the Force of Affliction, and should be preferred over it.
  • Heals us 10% of the damage dealt (lv. 52+), very little even when spread.
  • Balance Sage counterpart: Sever Force.
Affliction
Basics
  • Long lasting DoT, spreadable by Death Field.
  • Does not pin target, sometimes good for DoT spread.
  • Victim takes great bonus damage from Force Leach.
Usages
  1. Whenever the target is not going to fall in a few seconds or so.
  2. DoT spreading by Death Field or manually.
  3. To start a fight when all foes are melee, so that they zerg us as a group for easy DoT spread.
  4. Before Force Leach, when target would not be near death after Serenity.
Notes
  • Ticks immediately and once per 3 seconds for 18 seconds, 7 ticks total.
  • Deals internal (yellow) damage, which bypass many defences.
  • Double Force cost of Creeping Terror but slightly less damage.
  • Heals us 10% of the damage dealt (lv. 52+), very little even when spread.
  • Sage counterpart: Weaken Mind.
Death Field
Basics
  • Biggest and instant AoE. Spread Creeping Terror and Affliction.
  • Put counted debuff on victims that boost DoT damage and refund Force.
  • Also put a debuff that increases AoE damage taken.
Usages
  1. Spread Creeping Terror and/or Affliction to every target if any target have them.
  2. Spread both DoTs if possible, but if the target is flimsy and will die before that, DoT(s) a different target or just fire Death Field as burst AoE (without any DoT).
  3. Use on cooldown. Group, Single, Bursting, Kiting, just cast it on CD.
Notes
  • Newly spread DoTs will have full duration, but existing DoTs won't be refreshed.
  • Newly spread DoTs will immediately tick, adding nicely to burst damage.
  • Consumes multiple Recklessness when used on multiple targets. Not optimal but acceptable.
  • Heals us 20% of total damage dealt. (10% before lv. 52)
  • Deals internal (yellow) damage, which bypass many defences.
  • At level 68, Resonant Pulse has 50% proc on each target a low damage to itself and all enemies in 5 meters of it.
  • The pulse will not hit Lift and other mez.
  • Balance Sage counterpart: Force in Balance.
Force Leach
Basics
  • Biggest burst attack, also our main self-heal.
  • 30% more damage (and heal) against weakened target.
  • Force cost -15 when used after Death Field.
Usages
  1. Use on cooldown, after weaken mind and Death Field.
  2. Skip Death Field if it is on cooldown.
  3. Force Lightning instead if target is too weak to waste Weaken + Serenity.
  4. Best used of Recklessness because of high damage and heal.
Notes
  • Heal us 100% of damage dealt. (50% before lv. 52) If we need the heal it doubles the threat.
  • Deals internal (yellow) damage, which bypass many defences.
  • Don't need to be used immediately after Death Field; the force cost discount depends on its counted DoT debuff, which takes at least 4 GCD to spent.
  • Balance Sage counterpart: Force Serenity.
Force Storm
Basics
  • Primary weapon of mass destruction against flimsy enemies.
  • 33% chance to stun each standard and weak target per tick.
  • Zero cooldown and very big radius. But slow start, unmovable, and high Force cost.
Usages
  1. Stun and dispatch a big groups of flimsy or near-death opponents.
  2. Always use Death Field first: bursty, cheaper, and applies AoE debuff.
  3. Do not use on non-trivial foes; DoTs and focus fire them.
Notes
  • 3 seconds channel, ticks once per second.
  • Roughly equals to saber striking every target, at a high Force cost.
  • Sage counterpart: undefined.
Lightning Strike
Basics
  • Situational rotation filler. Primed by Force Lightning like Demolish.
  • Low damage and take time to cast when unprimed, worse than Shock.
Usages
  1. When Lightning Strike is primed, and Demolish is on cooldown longer than 3 seconds, and we can Force Lightning in time to prime and Demolish, and we have nothing else to do like self Bubble or self Resurgense.
  2. In all other cases, just Force Lightning.
Notes
  • At level 40, has 60% chance to trigger Rippling Force which immediately deals around ⅕ damage.
  • Sage counterpart: Disturbance.
Shock
Basics
  • The initial ranged attack of Sith Inquisitor, still useful at high level.
  • Moderate instant damage, quick cooldown, stuns standard and weak enemy 0.5s.
Usages
  1. When we are running and want to attack, and other attacks are on cooldown.
  2. Quickly stun a standard or weak enemy to stop its movement, channel, or cast.
  3. To spend last Recklessness on, when Death Field and Force Leach are both on cooldown.
Notes
  • Force Lightning is often a better choice (even when kiting), because of Throw's buffs and lack of CD. A primed Demolish is worth 4 Projects.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Project.
Saber Strike
Basics
  • Our first and only melee attack, we have outgrown it as a DPS.
  • For practical purpose, just forget Saber Strike ever exists.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: undefined.
Controls
Basics
  • Lift furthest foe(s), stun strongest, interrupt all interruptible boss.
  • Control (mez, stun, and interrupts) is our responsibility as a DPS.
  • Tanks are busy, healers lack accuracy. Control is our duty.
  • We lack in interrupts, but have some of the best controls.
Whirlwind
Basics
  • The best soft crown control (mez) in game.
  • Works on any controllable targets, including droids and turrets.
  • Breaks on any damage, including DoT or passive proc damage.
  • Very, very powerful with utilities.
Usages
  1. Take tough target(s) out of action while dealing with its more fragile friends.
  2. Take fresh target(s) (before DoT) out of action for a break (self heal, cooldown etc.)
  3. Disturb (inc. interrupts) kiting target(s) without pushing it away or Overload is on cooldown.
Notes
  • Last 8 seconds in PvP.
  • Starred NPCs (strong or above) recovers hp when lifted, but totally worth it.
  • Kite the other enemies away (5 meter+) to avoid Resonant Pulse hitting the target(s).
  • After we lift the furthest foe a few times, smart teammates will learn to expect it and leave them alone.
  • All op bosses, most fp bosses, and many ch bosses are immune.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Force Lift.
Overload
Basics
  • On of the three pushes in game and arguably the best (when used correctly).
  • Push all enemies in the 135° cone away from us, usually ahead, but adjacent ones may go backward or sideways.
  • Thus, when positioned correctly, Overload can push both ways on bridges.
Usages
  1. Push enemies down high cliffs and bridges.
  2. Push widespread enemies close together for easier AoE and DoT spread.
  3. Disturb multiple enemies, stopping their casts, channels, and movements.
  4. Do not do the last while in a team; it'd nullify teammates' attacks if the targets are pushed out of range.
Notes
  • Overload's push distance is the lowest of all three.
  • Cone goes towards character facing, not camera facing.
  • Pushing enemies to a wall is a easy way to line them up, but will result in a wide line if standing too close when push.
  • On some PvE maps the NPCs won't die from the fall; wait for them to respawn and dispatch normally (don't push again).
  • All op bosses, most fp bosses, and many ch bosses are immune.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Force Wave.
Electrocute
Basics
  • A hard stun that is not interrupted by damages.
  • Use on the toughest enemy in a group, esp. when it casts or channels.
  • Generally speaking, stun often and stun early in PvE.
Usages
  1. I usually stun after DoT spread on trash mobs, when I switch to single target focus fire.
  2. Also serves as an alternative interrupt, just with a long cooldown.
Notes
  • Watch teammate's stun. Overlapped stuns is a waste.
  • In a quick fight we usually don't need to stun.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Force Stun.
Jolt
Basics
  • Our basic interrupt ability, stops the target's current cast or channel.
Usages
  1. Use on big attacks of any interruptible champions and bosses. Very important.
  2. Also work great on trash mob healers (medics/repair bots).
Notes
  • Non-controllable enemies may still be interruptible.
  • Lift, Stun, Wave, and Jolt also work as interrupts on controllable enemies.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Mind Snap.
Healings and Defenses
Basics
  • Not our main job, unless there are kolto stations where we can stand by while we attack.
  • As ranged DoT DPS, run away and hide behind cover is a great damage control that we must get into habit of.
  • Unnatural Preservation is our main self heal and is free. Force Leach is our 2nd biggest attack and self heal. Use them.
  • Bubble is our most efficient "heal". Throw it out when a friend needs help.
  • After combat, revive fallen mates and heal them. We can revive in combat, but let healer do that.
  • When necessary, don't forget Heroic Moment, Unity/Sacrifice (gets from Light/Dark V), Medpaks, and Shielding adrenal.
Notes
  • Since we are not healer, our heals generate full threat.
  • Healings generate threat on every enemy, drawing their fire if no one else has threat, in which case Cloud Mind won't save us.
  • Absorption is no longer bugged; multiple absorbs run on a first come first absorb rule.
  • We can protect and heal friendly NPC under attacks, potentially trolling Imperials in PvE instance.
Force Barrier (Globe of Invulnerability)
Basics
  • The most powerful panic button in swtor.
  • Purge and immune to all removable negative effects.
  • Any movement or action will break the channelling.
  • Our DoTs keeps ticking while we are safe from harm.
Usages
  1. Use before we die, if there is hope left to win or flee.
  2. Can also be used as stun breaker, e.g. to save a friend.
  3. Break early when the danger has passed.
Notes
  • Can't stop instant death attacks and mechanics.
  • Cooldown not shortened by alacrity, but a utility does.
  • Post effect shield absorbs 15k per count (max 4) and last 5 seconds.
  • The utility that adds healing can heal us up to 28% hp.
  • (Test tick rate and alacrity)
Static Barrier (Bubble)
Basics
  • Our best damage control, absorbs about 10% hp.
  • More than all our heals except Unnatural Preservation.
  • Can be placed on self and all friend(s).
  • Comes with Force Imbanaced debuff which prevent re-bubble within 15 seconds.
Usages
  1. Keep it on ourselves always, except:
  2. If a Corruption Sorcerer is on the team likes to bubble us, let him/her do it. Corruption bubbles are stronger, and heal us on pop.
  3. If there is no Sorcerer healer or if (s)he isn't bubbling, we can bubble the team before combat.
  4. Throw a bubble over when a teammate is taking a beating.
Notes
  • Bubble is not heal, and will not crit or be affected by healing bonus/penalties.
  • When placed on teammate, absorbed damage is their threat, not our threat.
  • Bubble utilities only apply to own bubble, and will be visually different.
  • When we see these special bubble on an ally, do not bubble him/her.
  • Sage counterpart: Force Armor.
Unnatural Preservation
Basics
  • Our biggest heal, free and off GCD, heals only ourselves.
  • Heals slightly more than bubble right out of box.
  • With utilities, heals around ¼ hp on crit.
Usages
  1. Whenever hp is lower than ¾.
  2. Unnatural Preservation is off GCD, we can use it between other actions without slowing down.
  3. Often used with Recklessness to make sure it crit.
Notes
  • Cooldown not shortened by alacrity, but a utility exists.
  • Generate a big, instant threat.
  • Sage counterpart: Force Mend.
Resurgense
Basics
  • Our basic heal, low cooldown and cost efficient.
  • Heals about 5% over 9s, slightly above half a bubble.
  • Our only instant heal that can be used on friends.
  • We enjoy 10% defense bonus when rejuvenating, around 5% damage reduction.
Usages
  1. When attack can't be made and bubble is not an option, such as kiting.
  2. Pre-combat defence buff. Note that this will aggro all NPCs when we are hurt, get healed, and thus create threats.
  3. Keep it (and bubble, and Unnatural Preservation) up in a long fight to mitigate damage.
  4. Post-combat recovery. Use before Seethe.
  5. To "ping" or "touch" a friend out of combat.
Notes
  • Tick once per 3 seconds, three ticks total after upfront heal.
  • May be used on CD. Do 30% more heals on cooldown than end-to-end.
  • Upfront heal will consume Recklessness which is very wasteful. The HoT ticks do not.
  • Resurgense heals very little but gives a lasting off-heal impression because of HoT.
  • Sage counterpart: Rejuvenate.
Dark Heal
Basics
  • Our "burst" heal, no cooldown but casted and pretty expensive.
  • Roughly double the initial heal of Resurgense, but still less than bubble.
  • No pushback mitigation, don't use when under fire.
Usages
  1. When there is absolutely nothing else to do, almost never in combat.
  2. Post-combat patch up of friends after revive and Resurgense.
Notes
  • Our biggest repeatable Force burner.
  • Sage counterpart: Benevolence.
Cloud Mind
Basics
  • Threat dump. Lower our threat and hopefully drives our attackers away.
  • Useful even when solo. Let companion share the burden!
Usages
  1. Should be used as part of rotation, before we get focused fired.
  2. When we are attacked while there is a tank nearby, try to give the tank some time before dumping threat.
  3. Use after big hitters for best result.
Notes
  • Threat dump does not work in PvP, of course.
  • There is a utility that make it serves as a DCD.
  • Try to learn how threat works.
  • Don't use when we are kiting a boss on purpose, e.g. Dread Master Raptus in Dread Palace's final fight.
Expunge
Basics
  • Our main cleanse, removes up to two debuffs from self or friend.
  • Can cleanse all force-based debuffs and some non-force debuffs.
  • Which non-force debuffs are cleansable is more or less unpredictable. Ask EAware to fix.
Usages
  1. When the healer is not cleansing or need a hand (too many to cleanse or too busy healing).
  2. When we are on our own and need a cleanse, obviously.
  3. When a cleanse is very deadly and worth throwing cleanses to removed faster, e.g. Athiss final boss.
Notes
  • Don't bother to cleanse if the debuff is minor, like small burns or common armor reduce.
  • Try if you are unsure anyway. The pin in Rakata mm fp is debuffable, for example.
  • Force Barrier can also purge debuffs.
  • Sage counterpart: Restoration.
Unbreakable Will
Basics
  • Our basic stun breaker. Way too long cooldown.
  • Removes all slows, pins, and stunning debuffs from ourself.
Usages
  1. When we need to break out of stun.
  2. I normally reserve it for "break or die" situations, because the cooldown is so long.
Notes
  • Don't bother to break if the stun is minor, like slows or short stuns.
  • Many PvE pins and stuns are avoidable or interruptible.
  • Force Barrier, on an even longer cooldown, also serves as a stun breaker.
  • Stun breaker are off-gcd: we can and should act immediately on break.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Force of Will.
Reanimation
Basics
  • In-combat revive, limited to Sorcerer, Scoundrel, and Commando.
  • Can revive fallen ally or companion.
  • Prevent the whole team from in-combat revival for 5 mins.
Usages
  1. When the healer falls, we should pull him/her up as soon as feasible.
  2. When someone else falls, and the healer is busy, we may consider doing it.
  3. When in doubt, leave it to the healer. (S)he may be reserving it for more important teammates. Which may be you. Don't rob yourself of your future revive chance.
Notes
  • Our out of combat revive has no cooldown, and we should help revive after combat.
  • Sage counterpart: Revival.
Seethe
Basics
  • Out of combat Health/Force recovery.
  • Resurgense before rest to speed up hp recovery.
  • Heal wounded friends when we don't need to rest.
Notes
  • Try to rest as few and as short as possible when in a group.
  • Jedi Consular counterpart: Meditation.
Mobilities, Buffs, and Other Abilities
Basics
  • We have high mobilities, a unique friend pull, and a powerful raid buff.
  • Combined with heals, bubble, push, lift etc., we are very good at supporting our team.
Force Speed
Basics
  • Our mobile ability to chase enemy, kite, or flee.
  • Arguably our most important survival power.
Usages
  1. Whenever we need a boost of speed to be in the right place at the right time.
  2. Offensive example: Dash up to enemies and push them off the bridge or platform, before they run up to us.
  3. Defensive example: Dash behind cover after applying DoTs. Run outside of the range/area of a casted/channeled power.
  4. Non-combat example: Make it easier to jump past some gaps. (e.g. The False Emperor)
Notes
  • A utility can remove slow & immobilizing effects, must take in some fights and PvP.
  • Make sure it's worth the GCD. Force Speed + Overload cost 2+ GCDs; would Force Storm or Death Field be more efficient?
Phase Walk
Basics
  • First use: Set mark at current position (0.5 casted). Second use: Teleport back to mark.
  • Right click phase walk buff to remove placed mark.
  • Ignores line of sight and all obstacles; only limit is range. (60 meters)
Usages
  1. Teleport to behind cover, make the enemies chase us with DoTs burning.
  2. Mark and teleport to an important location in a boss fight (kolto station, console etc.).
  3. Mark a solid ground before/when fighting a pushy boss, as a safety net.
  4. When collecting datacron, place a mark before jump and teleport back if the jump failed.
Notes
  • Placing mark has no cooldown. Teleport cooldown not shortened by alacrity, but a utility does.
  • Not usable when hindered; trying to used it while hindered (ability greyed out) will cancel placed mark.
  • Mark is fixed in space and won't move with the terrain it is placed on, e.g. elevators or Tatooine Jawa balloon.
  • Except where the whole map is moving, e.g. Dark Design Heroic or Umbara flashpoint.
Extrication
Basics
  • The no.1 trolling ability in swtor.
  • Pulls a teammate to our location and lower his/her threat instantly.
  • Requires line of sight, but not consent. Subject can be immobilised.
Usages
  1. Pull companion or teammate from a dangerous or stuck position, e.g. rocket launch of Cademimu.
  2. Pull a teammate to an advantageous spot, such as shortcut, safe attack spot, or the cover where everyone else is doing LoS with.
  3. Pull a teammate who's pushed/pulled far away or to a tough spot, e.g. from a ledge or a different floor.
  4. Pull a near death teammate away from the heat of battle, hopefully causing target swap due to threat dump.
  5. Troll a teammate by pulling him/her into pit, cliff, fire, or other exciting terrains.
Notes
  • Can be used on friendly non-teammate but only reduce threat, not pull.
  • Can pull a falling friend in theory. Never see it happens in real combat. Always too slow.
  • Don't pull the tank unless (s)he is really stuck. Pulling will lower his/her threat resulting in lost control.
  • Don't pull if it is not obviously advantageous. It disrupts the subject's action, esp. melee classes.
  • Like all abilities, does not work on fallen comrades. A pity.
  • Sage counterpart: Rescue.
Recklessness
Basics
  • First of our two basic buffs, grant next two direct hits or heals +60% crit chance.
  • Yields big number when used with 1) Unnatural Preservation, 2) Force Leach on weakened victim.
Usages
  1. When we need that critical Unnatural Preservation, immediately.
  2. Before we put Death Field and Force Leach on a tough single target.
Notes
  • This power is why we should get 40% crit right after 110% accuracy. It turns Recklessness into 100% crit, or super crit, where overflowed crit chance turns into bonus heal/damage.
  • Don't waste it on Force Storm, Overload, Resurgense, Stun, and other small but direct heals/attacks.
  • Best used during Polarity Shift because of reduced cooldown.
  • The 6-set bonus adds 1 more charge for a total of 3. If we don't use Recklessness on CD, the set bonus is wasted!
  • Sage counterpart: Force Potency.
Polarity Shift
Basics
  • Second of our two basic buffs, boost action speed and hasten DoTs applied in buff duration.
  • Faster cast/channel and quicker ability cooldowns when used in the buff duration.
  • Also increase Force regen, but too little to make a difference.
Usages
  1. Speed up rotation and rotation cycle to increase dps.
  2. When things go south and we need to kill adds fast or bubble everyone fast.
  3. When impatient. The cooldown is short enough to be used every combat or two, and multiple times in longer boss fights.
Notes
  • Hastened DoTs won't slow down after buff expires.
  • Best used on a fresh rotation so that the cooldowns aligns.
  • Best used with Unlimited Power.
  • Sage counterpart: Mental Alacrity.
Unlimited Power
Basics
  • Raid buff. One of the best in game.
  • Apply to the whole team, and prevent further Unlimited Power for 5 mins.
Usages
  1. For bosses that have burn phase, use at burn phase.
  2. Otherwise, use early in a boss fight.
  3. When in doubt, use when boss is near death (10% hp or so), or when someone use their raid buff.
  4. Not used in non-boss fights, except when the party may be overwhelmed. Like when fighting multiple pods.
Notes
  • Cooldown not shortened by alacrity.
  • Only healie classes and sentinels has raid buffs, 4 total.
  • Unlimited Power is the only that is free and can be used anytime.
  • Sage counterpart: Force Empowerment.
Consuming Darkness
Basics
  • Spend a GCD to sacrifice force regen for instant force.
  • Force Lightning is almost always a better choice.
Usages
  1. Don't use this power. Forget it exists.
  2. If often run out of force, revise rotation habit; Madness Sages depends on synergies to save Force.
  3. Ok, may be when we are hiding and have nothing else to do.
Notes
  • Each use has a net gain of 20 Force, but we are wasting that GCD.
  • A full Force Lightning regens 28+ Force (36+ with 4-set bonus), while dealing damage and priming Demolish/Lightning Strike.
  • Consuming Darkness is the main Force regen ability of Seers. When we respec we need it on the bar and easy to click.
  • Sage counterpart: Vindicate.

Leveling and Gearing
Leveling
Basics
  • Low levels: Use Force Storm and Death Field to dispatch minions, then Force Lightning/Shock remaining foe(s). Use Lift/Stun to pause tough NPCs.
  • Mid levels: With instant Mind Crust at lv 24 and DoT spread at lv 28, we can meaningfully contribute in veteran flashpoints, a good way to learn and level up.
  • Level 42+: With Force Leach, we may use the Very Simple Rotation, but take away the final Force Lightning.
  • Level 56+: Force Lightning now ticks fast so can add the droped Throw back into rotation.
Notes
  • Free to play is limited to level 50. Subscribe once to have access to current level cap forever. Amazon and Origin sell cheaper packs than official site.
  • When a new expansion lands, again just subscribe once to raise to new level cap.
  • Many veterans stop playing much class story after getting starship (or even before that); Heroics and Flashpoints levels up quicker.
  • Most world bosses do not require high level to hunt. If a group is forming on the planet you are on, join the fun!
Gearing
Basics
  • Typhon (Prologue): Don't buy any gear. Just do class quest and equip the best loots.
  • Fleet: Go to left hand side of Supplies section and buy a set of moddable (orangw) gear.
  • PvE stats: 110% accuracy, then Crit > Power > Mastery > Endurance. Leave alacrity until endgame.
  • Modifications: Versatile hilt/armoring, Lethal Mod (unlettered), Initiative Enhancements. No augments until end game.
  • Upgrade main hand hilt, off hand armoring, chest armoring, and legs armoring at level X. They have biggest impact on attack and defence.
  • Upgrade the rest whenever you like. I upgrade every two steps (8 to 10 levels).
  • Optionally, run veteran flashpoints to get purple gears at current level, requires subscription or artifact authorisation.
Notes
  • Same relic effects don't stack; don't use two relic that proc the same stat. I prefer proc relic over clicky relic; we have enough to click already.
  • 110% accuracy is required to always hit NPCs, to ensures instant Demolish from a complete Force Lightning. 105% in pvp for the same.
  • Not missing is also very important when you need that stun, mez, interrupt, or push to lands.
  • Get crit up after accuracy and over alacrity. At 40%, Recklessness guarantees 100% crit.
  • Alacrity has a stepped impact on GCD: 0% = 1.5s, 7.15% = 1.4s, 15.4% = 1.3s. Aim for these magic numbers, don't go below or between.
  • Skip defensive stats (defense, shielding, absorption). We don't have the abilities to make good use of them.
  • High endurance is not worth the mastery and power we lost. Always pick power and mastery over endurance, but after critical and alacrity.
Endgame Gearing
Basics
  • At level 70, we earn cxp to rank up Galatic Command, split into 4 tiers.
  • Each rank gives a command crate. Open crates to get corresponding end game gear with set bonus. Put augmentations on gold gears.
  • Power & mastery proc relics (Serendipitous Assault & Focused Retribution) are best since critical relic hits diminishing return.
  • At full 14x ir248 gear with 228 to 236 augments, we have 3654 to 3682 stat budget for Accuracy, Critical, and Alacrity, plus 214 Critical from Relics and Crystals, plus 339 with accuracy/critical stim.
  • 737 Accuracy gives us 110% after companion bonus (see notes). 2x Initiative Enhancement/Package/Device and 3x Accuracy Augment gives us 750 to 756.
  • Alacrity should be 703 or 1860, but 1860 leaves too little budget for Critical. 1x Quick Savant E/P/D and 5x Alacrity Augment gives us 711 to 721.
  • I haven't had time to test it, but some says Critical's diminishing return means we should spare 2 to 4 augments for Mastery or Power.
Notes
  • It is strongly advised to unlock all four class buffs and all five companion bonus first.
  • If we learn Biochem and train it to 600, we can craft (or buy) and use reusable consumables.
  • Aim for 110% accuracy, 40% crit, and 7.15% alacrity first. Optimal arrangement takes some effort to meet.
  • Accuracy/crit stim is a easy way to fill the stat budget, but may moves to mastery/power stim (Versatile Stim) once fully geared.
  • Set bonus - 2-set: Force Leach gives a +2% buff to damage; 4-set: Increase Force Lightning's Force regen, reduce Polarity Shift CD; 6-set: Add 1 charge to Recklessness.
  • Set bonus does not affect rotation, except perhaps when to use Recklessness.
  • Corruption (healer) has a different set with important bonus. For switching, wear 6 of our set and 1 healer set, then we only need to carry 5 healer gear to switch over.
  • PvP needs only 105% accuracy; I'd switch my earpiece out for other stats.
Galactic Command
Basics
  • Opening command crates at top of tier 1 to 3 (close to but before rank 80, 190, and 299) will give better drop rate.
  • Each crate grants Command Tokens (CT), legacy wide and can be used to purchase Tier 1 (ir230) equipments on the fleet's Tier 1 equipment vendor.
  • Disintegrate unwanted gear to get unassembled components (UC). PvP and GSF are the main UC sources.
  • We can turn ir230, ir236, and ir242 gears into unassembled pieces with many UC. i.e. Take an ir230 chest plus UC to get Tier 2 unassembled chest.
  • We also get unassembled pieces from operations. Take the pieces to Tier 2-4 vendor to turn them into gear. i.e. Tier 2 unassembled chest can be turned into any ir236 chest.
  • Only ir230, ir236, and ir242 gears can be upgraded. Keep them as an option, as drop rate of useful ir248 is very low.
  • When getting a gold armor/saber/focus that we already have, check whether its mod and enhancement can upgrade other gears.
  • Focus's armoring can be placed on other armors, but that armor will lost its set bonus.
Combat
Companion
Basics
  • Companion can be set to any role, which decides their abilities. For most fights, DPS role (same as us) make most combats fast and safe.
  • Companion abilities can be excluded from auto-cast by right clicking the ability. The green box will go dark.
  • When we disable non-attack abilities, they will spend more time on basic attack and may increase their dps.
  • DPS can stun, heal can cleanse, tank can pull; disable these to use them when we want to.
  • Use passive mode to move companion out of AoE in boss fights.
  • Use Heroic Effort to heal in hard fights, and Unity/Sacrifice if we have it.
  • Fallen companions, self or of a teammate, can be revived in-combat like a PC.
Notes
  • A companion with high influence rank fights pretty well. In heroic and flashpoint it is common to replace missing players with companions.
  • Gift influrence decrease as companion rank goes up. Dialogue and crew mission influrence do not. It's preferable to fill first 15 to 20 levels with gifts first.
  • Buy the companion gift bonus perks and lots of cheap $200 gifts ($250 for non-subs) from the fleet's trade section. Shift+Click to buy in batch.
Solo Tactic
Basics
  • Bubble and Resurgense self before fight starts. Optionally place a Phase Walk. Keep bubble up during the fight.
  • When under fire, run behind cover to force the enemies to run around it for easy AoE. This is called LoS.
  • Start fight by lifting the furthest or strongest enemy. LoS the rest, spread DoT, Quake the weaks and normals, focus fire the rest one by one. Interrupt and stun champions.
  • Stock up on med packs. Cheaper than dying. Use stim at (close to) max level. Use adrenal in tough fights.
  • Spread Damage over Time and Kite the enemies. They each get their own section.
  • Overload can be used more or less liberally, but try to group the enemies for AoE.
Team Tactic
Basics
  • Never go first in a group; we are ranged not melee. Always let companion or teammate attack first. (Except pure Sorcerer group. Rare, but happened to me.)
  • Move last or second last (around/before healer). Find and stay near cover before and during combat.
  • The more we lift, the more our teammates are aware that we'll lift.
  • Do not Overload unless we are very confident it'll not make the melee unhappy.
  • Use Cloud Mind often, even when we are not under attack, to keep our threat low.
  • Most solo tactics also apply in a team setting.
Damage over Time (DoT)
Basics
  • Our DoTs are heavy, often but not always our main damage output.
  • Spread Affliction and Creeping Terror to multiple foes to multiply our dps.
  • Usually spread by Death Field, but put them on one by one when can't.
  • After spreading DoT, use focus fire to take down enemy fast, i.e. Death Field and Force Leach.
  • When enemies are very weak or near death, just Quake, Throw, or Shock.
  • Enemies who hasn't been attacked by others will turn to us when we DoT them. Be ready to LoS or endure their fire.
Notes
  • Spreadable DoTs deals ¼ to ⅕ dps of focus fire dps on each target. The more the targets the higher the total dps.
  • Use LoS, push, or run into enemy pod to huddle up the enemies for easy spread.
  • If you don't spread, don't play Madness; play Lightning instead.
  • Our DoTs deals more damage on near death victims (hp <= 30%), but should be placed early when they're healthier.
  • Each damage is a "tick". Demolish ticks faster, the others slower.
  • High alacrity make DoTs ticks faster in a shorter duration, determined when placed.
  • DoT ticks are indirect damage - no Recklessness but trigger and enjoy proc relic individually.
  • Think our DoT feedback heals as relic proc chance instead of heals; they heal so little.
Kiting
Basics
  • Kiting is making opponent(s) run around chasing us but rarely or never catch us.
  • While they are chasing us, our DoTs and/or friends are hurting them.
  • Very low dps; kiting is a slow war of attrition used when we would otherwise die too fast to see it through.
  • Sorcerer DPSs can't facetank and depends on kiting to survive hard fights.
  • Run sideways when kiting, don't run forward or backward. This let us throws slows and DoTs and other instant attacks, but not too slow to escape.
  • Keep the action up even when moving. DoTs, Shock, Death Field, Slow, Stun, Bubble, Resurgense. Should rarely run out of things to do.
Notes
  • Kiting is about damage control, about lowering dtps to a winnable level.
  • Mainly for solo and PvP. In a team we should let tankies go tank.
  • Also applies when a boss is chasing us, because everyone else is down or mechanic reason.
  • Mandatory when soloing master chapter bosses. Very few exceptions.

Interface and keybinds

Some tips for setting up swtor interface for best Madness Sorcerer experience.

Interface
  • Go preferred or subscription. Preferred get four ability bars (sub six), enough for all class abilities plus a few more.You don't need to hotkey all abilities, but no class abilities are useless (except Saber Strike).
  • Use team interface for non-op teams. They show companion which may need our supports too.
  • Enable "Highlight own effects" in target debuff bar, and scale it up.It let us see whether our DoTs and debuffs are on the target.
  • Do the same to own and friends' debuff bars.With big debuff icons we can identify bubble/revive/raid-buff timer and cleansable debuffs.
  • Enable Focus Target and do the same. Use it to monitor our DoT on the boss and its actions.
  • I sort all buffs and debuffs - own, friends, foes - by total duration, so that the same effect always go to the same place.
  • Scale up target and focus target cast bars until you can easily see the action text.Target yourself and Medidate to see the bar in interface editor.
  • Global scaling is independent from component scales, and should be set before fine tuning individual blocks.
Keybindings
Basics
  • Move forward and strafe with keyboard, turn with mouse drag, attack with hotkeys, select target with mouse or hotkey.
  • Life savers like Jolt, Force Barrier, Phase Walk, med packs should be placed on quick and accurate keys.
  • Kiting abilities (DoTs, Death Field, Force Speed, Slow etc.) should be bound to a key we can press on the move.
  • Force Lightning, Stun, Lift, Bubble, Unnatural Preservation, Resurgense are used a lot, and should have secondary priority (e.g. Shift modified).
  • Dark Heal, Unlimited Power, Meditate, Extrication, Reanimation, Consuming Darkness these are rarely used and may be put on hard keys or no keys.
  • The rest such as Polarity Shift, Recklessness goes in between (e.g. Ctrl modified).
  • Don't need to hotkey every ability, but class abilities and combat abilities (Heroic Effort, medkits etc.) should be on the bars.
Notes
  • Controls and keybindings are very personal. Experiment to find the one that suit you.
  • I binds ESDF to move, (Shift/Ctrl)+AZXCVBGTR12345 to abilities, F1-F6 to DCDs, 6-0 to companion, `TabQW to targeting.
  • I also assign easy keys to bubble and heals so that I don't need to shuffle them when I respec to Corruption.
  • You may or may not go to this extreme; only you can decide what works best for you.

Glossary
Adds
Additional NPCs that arrive as reinforcement in the middle of a boss fight. Usually should be taken down asap.
AoE
Area of Effect, abilities that act on the desginated area instead of specific target(s). Usually the more the target the merrier. Some AoEs are continuous and need the target to stay inside (e.g. Force Storm), but most are not. AoE are always circle or cone.
Cast
Casted abilities require us to stand still for a while to finish casting and take effect. They can be interrupted by crowd controls, and pressing Esc, moving, or using another ability also abort the casting. Damage we take during cast will pushback the cast bar, thus increasing total cast time. Force Leach and Dark Heal are casted. Alacrity shorten most cast times, and passive/utilities may allow a casted ability to be used while on the move.
Channel
Channelled abilities require us to stand still to use and deliver its effect in stages. They can be interrupted by crowd controls, and pressing Esc, moving, or using another ability also ends them early. Force Lightning and Force Storm are channelled. Alacrity shorten most channel times, quickening their effects.
Cleanse
Healie's ability to remove up to two debuffs. Sages can clear Mental/Force debuffs and the other two can clear Physical/Tech. Healers can additionlly clear both Physical and Mental.
Cooldown (CD)
Most abilities has a coodown period of a few seconds to a few minutes during which it cannot be used again. Note that some abilities like Reanimation and med kits can only be used once per fight. Most cooldowns are non-fixed and can be reduced by Alacrity.
DCD
Defensive Cooldown. Powerful defensive abilities with a fixed cooldown. Sages' are Force Barrier, and Unnatural Preservation/Cloud Mind/Phase Walk with utilities. Resurgense and bubble can be always on, and are not normally considered DCD.
Debuff
Harmful effects applied to self, including as DoT, slow, damage vulnerability, and other bad status. Some debuffs can be removed by cleanse abilities, and almost all can be removed by purge.
DoT
Damage over Time. DoTs attacks "stick" on the target for their durations and deals damage at regular interval without requiring further actions. Each damage is called a "tick".
DPS
Has two meanings; in this guide, all caps "DPS" means characters in the damage role, and lowercase "dps" mean Damage Per Second, a key performance metric for these DPSs. High dps means high damage output. See http://ixparse.com/rating/ for endgame dps distribution in operations.
DTPS
Damage taken over Time. NPC damage is fixed, but when we use kiting to delay and scatter these damages, DTPS falls, giving us time to heal and our DoTs time to work.
Endgame
The part of the game that happens at close to max level, where gearing and skill improvement replaces rapid level and ability growth. Mainly PvP, master mode flashpoints/uprisings/chapters, a few high level planets, and operation in all modes.
(E)HPS
(Effective) Heal Per Second, a key performance metric for healers. High (e)hps means high healing. Overheal is counted into hps but not ehps. See http://ixparse.com/rating/ for endgame hps distribution in operations.
Flashpoint (fp)
Sometime called Dungeons, flashpoints are side-story team PvE activities for up to four players that take roughly half an hour to complete. A quick way to level up, meet new friends, and get social points. Story mode is for solo, Veteran mode is for learning, and master mode is a challenge.
Crowd Control (CC)
Abilities that disable enemies such as stun, mez, sap, and push (over cliffs). The literal definition, uncommon in the game, includes general pull, push, interrupts, LoS, and perhaps AoEs.
GCD
Global Cooldown. Most abilities put us on a short 1.5s cooldown during which we cannot use next ability. Alacrity can shorten GCD and thus increase action speed.
Heal
The act or role of healing, cleansing, and reviving teammates. Responsible for saving team from mistakes, critical in sustained fights.
Healie
Advanced classes that can spec into heal and have some healings even as DPS. The republic has Sorcerer, Smuggler, and Commando. Of these, smuggler is melee but can heal at range.
HoT
Heal over Time. HoTs "stick" on the target for their durations and heals at regular interval without requiring further actions.
LoS
Line of Sight. The technique of hiding behind covers (tall boxes, pillars, doorways, wall corners) to block their attacks through line of sight, and lure them to group up at the corner.
Mez
Short of mesmerize. Commonly known as soft CC, a kind of Crowd Control that disables the target for a long duration or until they take any damage. Some AoEs ignore mez and can be safely used, but ours are passive so we must take care when we AoE.
Off-Heal
Healie classes in DPS specs who can heal a bit. That means us. Off-heals lack lacks proper healing abilities and can only help a bit.
Off-Tank
Often used in an operation to refer to the non-main tank. Tanky classes are usually called off-tank too, but non-tank specs lack proper tanking abilities and can only help a bit.
Operation (op)
Also called Raid, operations are ultimate endgame PvE contents normally done in 8 or 16 person group, and require decent to very high skill and gear to complete.
Passives
Passive abilities that we get as we level up, such as Resurgense's defense bonus (lv. 12), Death Field's DoT spread (lv. 28), or faster Force Lightning (lv. 56).
Pin
Immobilise effects - effects that prevents the victim from moving. Unless it is a hindering effect, pins won't prevent escape by pull, jump, or teleportation.
Proc
Randomly triggered events, or the triggering of these events. Commonly seen on relics, but every class has some proc passives. Sometimes loosely used to describe all activated effects (random or not), such as saying Force Lightning procs Demolish.
Purge
An ability that removes all debuffs from self. For Sages it is Force Barrier.
PvE
Player verse Environment. The part of the game where we compete with NPC enemies instead of real players. Class missions, planet missions, heroics, flashpoints, uprisings, operations, and expasion chapters are all PvE.
PvP
Player verse Player. The part of the game where we compete with real players instead of NPC enemies. Duels, warzones, and open world PvP are all PvP.
Rotation
Rotations are ability sequences that lead to optimal synergies and outputs, such as dps or hps. Proc based discipline such as Lightning use a priority rotation. Others such as Madness use a repetitive or segmented rotation.
Sap
A special form of mez that can only be used in stealth and on an organic target. Usually used to bypass enemies without alerting them. We have none since we can't stealth.
Stun
Also known as Hard CC, a kind of Crowd Control that stops all actions of the target for a short duration. e.g. Shock can stun weak and standard enemies.
swtor
Star Wars: The Old Republic, somtimes simply Tor. A Star Wars MMO game set thousands of years before the movies, when Sith and Sith were more abundant and lightsabers less powerful.
Tank
The act or role of taking point and taunting enemies to draw their fire away from teammates. Tanks has many DCDs and usually stack defensive stats to endure the damage. The most heroic role in any team.
Tanky
Advanced classes that can spec into tank and have some taunts even as DPS. The republic has Guardian, Shadow, and Vanguard. All tankies are melee.
Threat
In PvE combat, every time we attack or heal we are threatening the NPCs, represented by creating a numeric threat. Each NPC keep a list of who is threatening it, a threat table, and will attack (aggro) the one who made the most threat.
There is no threat in PvP; players decide who to attack.
Tick
Each stage of channelled abilities, DoTs, and HoTs is a tick. Force Quakes has 4 ticks, 3 of which deals damage.
Trash Mobs
Non-boss fight NPC enemies. Usually a small group of low difficulty NPCs standing there waiting to be defeated.In map design terms they make moving between boss fights less boring, and make the boss fight more dramatic in comparison.Note that some (very rare) encounters have so much mobs that they are essentially boss fights, e.g. Landing Party Uprising's Zakuulan Ambush.
Uprising
No-dialogue team PvE activities for up to four players, like a short flashpoint. No less deadly but drop a lot less rewards so few players run them.
